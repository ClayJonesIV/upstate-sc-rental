UPSTATE SC MACRO VIEW

The Upstate South Carolina market is entering a period of stabilization after strong growth in 2022-2023. The regional average rent change year-over-year sits slightly negative at -1.1%, reflecting the wave of new apartment supply delivered across Greenville, Spartanburg, and the suburban corridor through 2024. This is a healthy correction, not a crisis — vacancy rates remain near long-term equilibrium and days on market are seasonal rather than structural.

CROSS-MARKET INVESTMENT THESIS

For new investors, Simpsonville stands out as the most defensible entry point. Its premium rents reflect genuine demand from Greenville professionals seeking suburban quality without downtown prices. Greer is the contrarian opportunity: BMW and Inland Port employment create durable demand underpinning a market that trades at a discount to Greenville proper.

OUTLOOK AND RISKS

Watch the new supply pipeline carefully through mid-2025. The single most important action for Upstate SC owners this month is to pull a current comp analysis before setting any renewal increases — the data suggests holding or modest increases of 2-3% will retain quality tenants while markets absorb new inventory.
